The Golden Age of Kali Yuga

Vaibhav Gautam

October 2020

We all are living in an age that is supposed to be the last Yuga, Kali Yuga. It is said that there are four different yugas. The First Yuga was Satya yuga. During this yuga the morals were high and everyone respected each other. People had pure souls and there was harmony and peace everywhere. People in this yuga performed good deeds and there were no such things as hate, jealousy, greed, anger, deceit and evil.

In this era, the Supreme Lord, Sri Maha Vishnu, took the first four of his avatars, namely, Matsya (the Fish), Kurma (the Tortoise), Varaha (the Boar) and Narasimha (the Half-Man Half-Lion form). The Treta Yuga is the second of four Yugas and it's beginning marks the ending of Satya Yuga.This is the era when Lord Vishnu took avatars like Vamana Avatara, Parshuram Avatara and Lord Rama Avatara. As evident from the stories we know related to these avatars, there was some evil introduced in this era.

There was also a decline in Dharma which worsened in the next Yuga, Dwapara Yuga. This Yuga is the third Yuga and Krishna Avatar of Lord Vishnu physically existed in this Yuga. This Yuga saw a very steep decline in Dharma and Morals and there are many stories of this yuga that supports this fact. It is also believed that the caste system came into existence during this Yuga and discrimination between people started.Higher class people gave no attention and support to lower caste people and they were often treated in bad ways. Violence also emerged in this age and people started having a competitive nature. Greed and Jealousy were common among people. Events tat took place during Mahabharata clearly shows that how a war started within a family and how it took so many lives. All the acts of injustice that took place during the end of Dwapara Yuga brought down the level of Dharma. Now you might have noticed a pattern here. With each age the level of Dharma decreases and now this is the final age, Kali Yuga.

“The magic of autumn has seized the countryside; now that the sun isn't ripening anything it shines for the sake of the golden age; for the sake of Eden; to please the moon for all I know.”

This is the worst of them all. It is believed that when Lord Krishna left his Physical Form in Dwapara Yuga, Kali Yuga started. I don't have to tell you much about this Yuga as we all are experiencing everything going on in this Yuga both physically and mentally. There are acts of violence taking place very frequently. Every day we read newspapers or watch news channels on our TVs, we come to realize that the world is getting more and more cruel.

At last !

At the end of this Yuga, the Purana states, Kalki would return to battle with the dark forces on earth. And this period is believed to be the Golden Age of Kali Yuga. After this, situation will get even more worse. People will commit evil deeds, knowledge will be lost and morals will perish. The First 10,000 years are said to be the Golden age of Kali Yuga and at least 4,000 years have already passed.

We all should perform good deeds and should never indulge ourselves in wrong activities. This way we can make sure that we will get rid of continuous life cycle and will finally attain Moksha. Many people will agree and may disagree. But that is the beauty of it, no one can disagree that we should perform good deeds, help others, bring positivity and stay away from bad company and influence. Whether you’re a believer or not, you will surely see the importance of these Yugas and what they teach us.
